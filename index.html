Elantris

Brandon Sanderson

Amazon Link "- Tagged with fiction, great writing"

""You will find that hate can unify people more quickly and more fervently than devotion ever could""
"There are more political battles than physical. Still the depth of the world and the liveliness of the characters will keep you interested in the story, as much as any magical battle would. The most interesting character in my opinion is one of the villains. I think Sanderson did a wonderful job of making him one of those villains that you can sympathize with. He is much more complex than either the hero or the heroine. Of course, he is not the only villain and the others are relatively typecast, but Sanderson knocked him out of the park. Another nice thing about this fantasy novel is that it is relatively self-contained. Too many fantasy novels are simply one piece of a bigger whole; whereas "Elantris" is a simple tale. It could however, be spun off into sequels if Mr. Sanderson wants (which I would love!). The biggest theme in this work, and one that I am seeing throughout Sandos novels, is self-reliance in both material and spiritual matters."
